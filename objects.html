Welcome to 2035.
Do we still use plastics?
Absolutely.
But the discovery of their toxicity, pervasiveness and degradation into micro and nano scales, has had major implications on how we use them, and more crucially, who uses them. This exhibition presents a cautionary tale for those before this time.
The
Protagonist
Veni
is someone who is at the bottom of the consumption spiral. She is a 27-year-old cook in a posh residential locality in New Delhi. She is in the third trimester of her first pregnancy, a precious pregnancy, now that they are difficult to achieve. Veni lives a life starkly in contrast to that of the people she works for, her didis. She lives in a matchbox house in M.S Kishan Garh, a model slum with her husband where she is preparing for her godh bharai (baby shower). This exhibition presents instances and objects from her life that tell the story of plastics in 2035.
